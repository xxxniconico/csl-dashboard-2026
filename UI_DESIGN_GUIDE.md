# UI Design Guide: CSL Dashboard 2.0 (Football Manager & High-End Sports Edition)

## 1. Design Philosophy
The goal is to move away from "Data Tables" and towards "Data Immersion." The UI must evoke the feeling of a professional football management interface (Football Manager) combined with the clean, authoritative aesthetic of premium sports journalism (The Athletic).

**Core Principles:**
- **Immersive Dark Mode**: High contrast, low eye strain, focus on data visibility.
- **Information Hierarchy**: Essential stats at a glance; deep details on demand.
- **Narrative-Driven**: Using visual cues (colors, icons) to tell the story of the season.
- **Minimalist Precision**: Clean typography and ample whitespace to prevent "data overwhelm."

### 2.2 Typography
- **Font Family**: `Inter`, `system-ui`, `-apple-system`, `sans-serif`.
- **Headings**: Bold, heavy weight, high contrast.
- **Data Values**: Monospaced or highly legible sans-serif (e.g., `JetBrains Mono` for scores) to ensure column alignment.

## 3. UI Components & Patterns

### 3. 1 The "Squad Card" (Team Component)
- A card representing a club.
- **Front**: Club logo (if available), Name, and Points.
- **Micro-stats**: Small, color-coded badges for `W-D-L` (e.g., `[W] [D] [L]`).
- **Interaction**: Clicking the card "expands" or "drills down" into the team's specific player stats.

### 3. 2 The "Match Ticker" (Live/Recent Results)
- A horizontal or vertical scrolling strip.
- Uses high-contrast score typography (e.g., `2 - 1`).
- Visual indicators for match importance or highlights.

### 3. 3 The "Stats Radar" (Player Component)
- Use `Chart.js` to render a Radar Chart for key players.
- Axes: Goals, Assists, Yellow Cards, Red Cards, Matches Played.
- Transparent fill with a glowing border.

### 3. 4 Navigation Sidebar
- A persistent, slim sidebar with icons (League, Players, Schedule, Settings).
- Collapsible to maximize data workspace.

## 4. Interaction & Motion (UX)
- **Progressive Disclosure**: Don't show all 50 player stats at once. Show the top 5, then provide a "View All" option.
- **Smooth Transitions**: Use CSS transitions for tab switching and card expansions.

- **Hover States**: Subtle lift effect (`transform: translateY(-4px)`) and border glow on cards.
